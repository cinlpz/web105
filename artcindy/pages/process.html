PROCESS

My art Process

When I create art, I use my iPad and Apple Pencil because it's easy for me. I focus mainly on digital art because it's convenient. I get ideas from other artists online, like on social media or Pinterest. I like to bring my characters to life, giving them personalities through their designs. Sometimes, I work on characters made by other artists that I "adopt." I use a program called Procreate and mostly stick to the default tools. Occasionally, I use a free brush pack called Jingsketch basics. I start with a light or heavy sketch, depending on what I'm drawing. Usually, I paint over the sketch, but sometimes I clean it up for neat lines. Then, I add flat colors and move on to lighting and shading. If I'm doing a painting, I focus on shades and colors first, worrying about details later. I use several layers, usually between 8 to 20 or more. If I'm making art for someone else, I touch up the final details using the liquify tool and add a background. But when it's for me, I often focus on character designs and skip making backgrounds since I keep these designs for myself.
